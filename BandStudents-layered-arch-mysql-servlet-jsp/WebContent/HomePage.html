Mar'Kaja Ramirez, a senior biology major from Natchez, Mississippi, was recently named the band's head drum major, a position that Ramirez has strived to achieve since her freshman year. "It is an amazing feeling because I've worked hard to get to this point," said Ramirez. "I'm happy to have finally accomplished my goal." The position allows Ramirez to further exercise her love for performing. She gets joy from the positive reactions from spectators who are wowed by her skills.

The achievement didn't come without some adversity. After struggling to make the squad in her freshman year, her effort wasn't enough in her sophomore campaign, as she failed to make the team. She remembers the pain of rejection, and how that motivated her to work twice as hard. "You have to work your way up the ranks. My freshman year was, by far, the hardest, but being a part of a male-dominated drum section prepared me to follow my dreams. I didn't make it my sophomore year, which was a hard pill to swallow, but it pushed me to work harder."
